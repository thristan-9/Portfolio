About me

I am Thristan.

My first contact with code was 5 years ago as an intern at a startup called Clinicorp, a Saas company with more than 70,000 users that helps clinics self-manage. When I joined, there were around 15 employees in the entire company and that was great for me, as there was almost no development process and everything was very rustic, I had to learn a bit of everything before I could start delivering value to the company.

During this 5 years, I was responsible for many full stack projects such as recurring billing, import of financial data, bank account interface and much more.

After a while, I began to realize that just seeing URLs in the postman, SQL queries and terminals didn't captivate me as much as creating beautiful interfaces with code and a good user experience. So I started dedicating myself to getting good at it.

The best experience that I had

Was planning and creating a complex interface that allowed clinics to set up a bank account within the system. The client had to send some data, documents and selfie from clinic partners through the system, a team validated the data and documents through an internal platform, and if everything was in order, we sent the data to Dock, a white-label banking company(Baas), once the account was created the client could manage it through our app. I was responsible for the frontend of this project, at the beginning it was very challenging as there were some things I had never done before, but the project started to structure itself and in the end it was an incredible feeling to see everything working as it should.

The coolest thing about this project, thinking in terms of user experience, was that if the user doesn't have all the photos of the documents (front and back) and the partner's selfie to upload to the form, and normally nobody does, they can choose an option that sends a link via SMS to the chosen phone number, with this link we request access to the camera and then they can take photos of the document and selfies, that are automatically saved in our database.
